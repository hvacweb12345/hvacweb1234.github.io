LOLER Examinations: Safeguarding Access Equipment and Ensuring Compliance

  • Matt Smith
  • August 31, 2023

Introduction Safety is paramount in the realm of elevated access equipment, and one crucial component of safety is LOLER (Lifting Operations and Lifting Equipment Regulations) examinations. In this comprehensive post, we'll delve deep into the world of LOLER examinations, shedding light on why they are indispensable for maintaining your access equipment's condition and, ultimately, ensuring the safety and compliance of your operations.

Safety is non-negotiable when it comes to elevated access equipment. The well-being of your operators and the successful execution of your projects hinge on the reliability and safety of your equipment. That's where LOLER examinations come into play. In this blog post, we'll explore the critical role of LOLER examinations in safeguarding your access equipment and ensuring compliance with essential regulations.

Understanding LOLER Compliance

LOLER, or Lifting Operations and Lifting Equipment Regulations, is a set of regulations designed to ensure the safe use of lifting equipment, including access equipment like cherry pickers and scissor lifts. Complying with LOLER is not only a legal requirement but also a fundamental aspect of maintaining safety standards in your workplace.


A Closer Look at LOLER Examinations

LOLER examinations are a vital part of LOLER compliance. These examinations involve a comprehensive assessment of your access equipment's condition, focusing on critical safety components. LOLER examinations aim to identify potential issues before they become safety hazards.


How Frequently Should LOLER Examinations Be Conducted?
The frequency of LOLER examinations depends on several factors, including the type of equipment, its usage patterns, and the manufacturer's recommendations. Regular examinations are essential to ensure that your equipment remains safe for use.
